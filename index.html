Kakeibo Notes – Japanese Budgeting Method

Kakeibo is a traditional Japanese budgeting method focused on mindful spending and intentional saving. It encourages reflection before spending rather than relying on apps or automation.

What is Kakeibo?

Kakeibo originated in Japan in the early 20th century and uses simple pen-and-paper budgeting to help people become more aware of their financial habits.

Core Principles of the Kakeibo Method

  • Awareness before spending
  • Monthly reflection instead of daily tracking
  • Behaviour change over automation
  • Simple and intentional money management

Who is Kakeibo Best For?

Kakeibo is useful for people who feel overwhelmed by budgeting apps, want to reduce impulsive spending, and prefer mindful money habits.
